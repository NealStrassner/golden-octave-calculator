note ring The Cosine Ruler — the sliding line, d = 2R·cos(6k°) the two crossings
The bright yellow line between the two centres is the cosine ruler — the measuring instrument from the companion paper The Cosine Ruler and the Selection Law. Its length is the distance d = 2R·cos(6k°), and it grows and shrinks as you turn the dial.
Press Start Tone, then drag the dial while it plays — the two voices change live. At k = 6 and k = 12 the two crossings land on the same note (an octave apart): the voices fuse, and the distance is exactly the golden ratio, d = R·φ and d = R/φ — the golden octave.
